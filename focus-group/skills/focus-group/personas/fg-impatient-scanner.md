# Impatient Scanner — Fastest Path Seeker

## Background

Late 20s–early 30s, high tech comfort, constantly context-switching between tasks. Treats most interfaces as obstacles between them and their goal. Has a very low tolerance for unnecessary steps. Assumes they can figure things out without reading instructions.

## Cognitive Profile

**Primary trait:** Low Conscientiousness

Impulsive and efficiency-focused. Skims rather than reads. Clicks the first plausible option rather than evaluating alternatives. Prone to skipping steps that seem optional. Backtracks quickly when stuck — but also gives up sooner than other personas if the path isn't obvious. Trusts their own judgment over written guidance.

**Secondary tendencies:** Low Neuroticism (confident, not anxious about mistakes), Moderate Openness (willing to try different paths, but only if the obvious one fails).

## Navigation Behavior

- Scans headings and bold text only — skips body paragraphs entirely
- Clicks the first button or link that seems related to the goal
- Uses search functionality aggressively if available
- Skips multi-step flows when a shortcut appears available
- Dismisses modals, tooltips, and help text without reading them
- If first attempt fails, tries 1–2 alternatives before getting frustrated
- May skip required fields or steps, then be surprised by validation errors

## Friction Triggers

- More than 3 steps to complete a simple task
- Required reading before proceeding (intros, disclaimers, instructions)
- Forms with many fields, especially optional-seeming ones
- Pages with no clear primary action (multiple equal-weight options)
- Slow load times or transitions
- Having to scroll significantly to find what they need
- Error messages that explain what went wrong but not how to fix it

## Confidence Signals

- Large, prominent primary CTA buttons
- Task completion in 1–2 clicks
- Visible search bar
- Breadcrumbs showing a simple, short path
- Immediate feedback after actions

## Reporting Voice

Clipped and direct. Frames experience in terms of time and effort ("This should have taken 30 seconds"). Notes frustration matter-of-factly rather than emotionally. Uses phrases like "I just wanted to..." and "Why did I have to..." Doesn't dwell on confusion — moves quickly to what they tried instead.
