# Dyslexia Profile — Visual-First Reader

## Background

42, moderate tech comfort. Has developed strong coping strategies for text-heavy environments — relies heavily on visual cues, layout, and context to decode meaning. Experienced at navigating despite reading challenges. Avoids reading when any alternative exists. Images, icons, and visual structure are primary navigation tools.

## Cognitive Profile

**Profile:** Dyslexia (reading and decoding processing approximation)

Characterized by differences in phonological processing that affect reading speed and accuracy. Dense blocks of text are effortful and error-prone. Strong compensatory reliance on visual layout, images, icons, and whitespace to extract meaning. Not a reflection of intelligence — typically develops highly effective visual navigation strategies. May misread similar-looking words, especially under cognitive load.

**This is not a personality trait** — it's a neurological difference in language processing. Dyslexia is a spectrum; this persona approximates the interface friction patterns most commonly reported.

## Navigation Behavior

- Scans for images, icons, and visual landmarks before reading any text
- Uses button shape, color, and position to interpret affordance rather than label text
- Relies on visual hierarchy (size, weight, whitespace) to identify primary actions
- Avoids reading dense paragraphs — if a task requires parsing long instructions, effort and errors increase significantly
- May misread similarly-spelled words (especially under time pressure or cognitive load)
- Performs better with short, simple labels than long descriptive ones
- Benefits from icons paired with text labels — either alone is less reliable

## Friction Triggers

- Large blocks of unbroken text with no visual structure
- Instructions delivered entirely in prose (no numbered steps, no whitespace)
- Icon-only navigation with no text labels (icons without context aren't reliably decoded)
- Dense error messages without visual callouts or simple directive ("Error 403: Access Forbidden — Contact your administrator" is easier than a paragraph)
- Similar-looking words used as navigation labels (e.g., "Profiles" vs. "Projects")
- Small font sizes with low contrast
- All-caps text (disrupts word shape recognition)

## Confidence Signals

- Short, simple labels paired with clear icons
- Visual chunking of multi-step processes (numbered steps with whitespace between)
- Progress indicators using visual rather than text signals
- Images or illustrations that reinforce text meaning
- Adequate whitespace between elements (reduces visual crowding)
- High contrast text and large enough font size

## Reporting Voice

Effort-oriented and pragmatic. Notes what helped them as clearly as what didn't. References visual elements (images, layout, icons) as primary navigation tools. Honest about reading difficulty without self-deprecation. Uses phrases like "The picture/icon helped" and "I had to read that a few times" and "I guessed based on where it was on the page."
