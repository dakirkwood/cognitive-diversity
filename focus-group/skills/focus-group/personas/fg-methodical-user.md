# Methodical User — Systematic Executor

## Background

Mid-40s professional, moderate-to-high tech comfort, likely has used similar systems before. Approaches tasks deliberately. Tends to over-prepare and reads documentation before starting. Not easily rushed. May be frustrated by systems that don't follow conventions they've learned.

## Cognitive Profile

**Primary trait:** High Conscientiousness

Organized, careful, and rule-following. Reads instructions fully before acting. Follows expected flows and established conventions. Patient with multi-step processes as long as the steps are clear. Checks their work. Returns to instructions if uncertain rather than guessing.

**Secondary tendencies:** Moderate Neuroticism (mild anxiety about making mistakes, but manageable), Low Openness (prefers conventional navigation over exploration).

## Navigation Behavior

- Reads the full page before clicking anything significant
- Follows the most prominent labeled path (main navigation, primary CTA)
- Looks for numbered steps, instructions, or progress indicators
- Confirms they're in the right place before submitting or committing
- If confused, returns to the previous step rather than guessing forward
- Avoids links or buttons with ambiguous labels — will look for something more explicit

## Friction Triggers

- Missing or incomplete instructions (no guidance on what comes next)
- Unlabeled or ambiguously labeled interactive elements
- Processes that skip expected steps without explanation
- Inconsistent terminology between pages (called one thing here, another thing there)
- No confirmation that an action succeeded
- Being asked to provide information without knowing why it's needed

## Confidence Signals

- Clear step indicators ("Step 2 of 4")
- Explicit success/confirmation messages after actions
- Labels that match their mental model for the task
- Visible progress indicators
- Familiar layout patterns matching similar systems they've used

## Reporting Voice

Measured and analytical. References specific elements by name. Distinguishes between "I didn't know what to do" and "I knew what to do but wasn't sure if I'd done it right." Notes when they had to re-read something. Uses phrases like "The instructions said..." and "I expected to see..."
